#monetisation 

As a multiplayer co-op game, some basic aspects of the monetisation are set in stone:
- It must be free-to-play, to allow for friend groups to easily pick up the game
- It cannot limit missions/events to paying customers, or make a long-time player unable to smoothly introduce a newcomer
- It should encourage multiplayer gameplay
- It should incentivise frequently playing the game, but avoid making the player "log in for dailies"

Additionally, for the sake of player retention, and avoiding "spending spree burnout":
- It should have a "spending cap"; players cannot be allowed to splurge on the game in amounts larger than $50 a week
- Purchases should be split between "anchors", "hooks", and "novelty"
	- **Hooks:** purchases that entice the player to *start* spending; high-value and low-stakes.
	- **Anchors:** purchases that, once bought, encourage the player to come back to the game; these are e.g. permanent boosts, and fancy cosmetics; Hook purchases should lead smoothly into these items.
	- **Novelty:** Miscellaneous cosmetics and the like, providing additional, optional content; these do not have to be necessary, and can be suited to niche tastes, so as to allow and encourage small, personalised purchases.
- As a free-to-play game, there should be a small amount of permanent, high-value purchases that the game "expects" the player to buy in the long run, totalling around in price to $40-50; The *gameplay* should be enjoyable for a non-spending player, but long-term operations should be based around the $40-50 spending mark.

Characters are a driving force behind the monetisation, but the flow is not direct - consult [[Character Acquisition]] for a deeper look at the intrinsics.
