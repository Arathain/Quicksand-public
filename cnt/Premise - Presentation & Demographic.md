- Skill expression should be encouraged and rewarded, but not required to enjoy the game; difficult content should be optional, but significant.
- Co-op action games are usually marketed towards a primarily male audience - increasing marketing towards a female demographic could prove fruitful, if audience research suggests that the genre can find root in new grounds.
- "A game for everyone is a game for no-one"; with that said, effort should be put into compartmentalising aspects of the presentation; individual designs should strive to please *specific* parts of the audience, instead of being universally-beloved. By constantly pushing at the edges of the design space, the game should minimise design overlap, and entice new players while maintaining old ones.
- **Design Space:** Level/background geometry should have reduced contrast compared to enemies, VFX, and the player; lowering saturation in the background to allow character and enemy VFX to pop is crucial for maximising readability; good examples include e.g. Zenless Zone Zero's in-action presentation.
